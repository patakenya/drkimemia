Frequently Asked Questions

The DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) diet is a flexible and balanced eating plan that helps create a heart-healthy eating style for life. It’s rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low-fat dairy foods; includes meat, fish, poultry, nuts, and beans; and limits sugar-sweetened foods, red meat, and added fats.

If your blood pressure is normal (less than 120/80 mm Hg), check it every year or more often as your doctor suggests. If elevated, your doctor may recommend more frequent checks. Home monitoring can also help manage your blood pressure.

Remember the acronym FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency services. Other signs include sudden numbness, confusion, trouble seeing, difficulty walking, or severe headache.
