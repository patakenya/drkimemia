Understanding Non-Communicable Diseases

Dive into the causes, symptoms, risk factors, and management strategies for major NCDs to empower yourself with knowledge for better health.

Heart Disease

Heart disease encompasses conditions affecting the heart's structure and function, such as coronary artery disease, heart failure, and arrhythmias. It’s the leading cause of death globally, claiming 17.9 million lives annually.

High Risk

Symptoms

  • Chest pain or discomfort (angina)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue, weakness, or irregular heartbeat

Risk Factors

  • High blood pressure, high cholesterol
  • Smoking, obesity, physical inactivity
  • Family history, diabetes

Prevention & Management

  • Eat a heart-healthy diet (e.g., DASH or Mediterranean)
  • Exercise regularly (150 min/week moderate activity)
  • Manage stress, quit smoking, and monitor blood pressure

Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic condition affecting blood sugar (glucose) processing, with Type 1, Type 2, and gestational variants. Over 537 million adults live with diabetes as of 2021.

Increasing Prevalence

Symptoms

  • Increased thirst and frequent urination
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue, blurred vision, slow-healing sores

Risk Factors

  • Obesity, physical inactivity
  • Family history, age (45+)
  • High blood pressure, gestational diabetes history

Prevention & Management

  • Maintain a healthy weight, balanced diet
  • Regular exercise (150 min/week)
  • Monitor blood sugar, take prescribed medications

Hypertension luminosity

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, occurs when the force of blood against artery walls is consistently elevated. An estimated 1.28 billion adults aged 30-79 have it, often undetected.

Often Undetected

Symptoms

  • Often asymptomatic ("silent killer")
  • Severe cases: headaches, nosebleeds
  • Shortness of breath, chest pain

Risk Factors

  • Age, family history, obesity
  • High salt intake, stress
  • Smoking, excessive alcohol use

Prevention & Management

  • Reduce salt, eat fruits and vegetables
  • Exercise regularly, maintain healthy weight
  • Monitor blood pressure, take medications as prescribed

Stroke

A stroke occurs when blood supply to the brain is interrupted, either by a blockage (ischemic) or bleeding (hemorrhagic), causing brain cells to die rapidly.

Medical Emergency

Symptoms

  • Sudden numbness or weakness (face, arm, leg)
  • Confusion, trouble speaking or understanding
  • Severe headache, dizziness, loss of balance

Risk Factors

  • Hypertension, smoking, diabetes
  • High cholesterol, obesity, age (55+)
  • Family history, atrial fibrillation

Prevention & Management

  • Control blood pressure, cholesterol
  • Quit smoking, exercise regularly
  • Seek immediate care for symptoms (FAST: Face, Arm, Speech, Time)

Take Control of Your Health

Understanding these diseases is the first step. Assess your risk, adopt preventive measures, and consult a healthcare provider for personalized advice.
