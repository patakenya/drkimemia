Preventing Non-Communicable Diseases

Proactive lifestyle changes and medical monitoring can significantly reduce your risk of heart disease, diabetes, hypertension, and stroke. Start your journey to better health today.

Prevention Guidelines

Adopt these evidence-based strategies to lower your risk of NCDs and improve your overall well-being. Small changes can make a big difference.

Lifestyle Modifications

1

Regular Physical Activity

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity (e.g., brisk walking) or 75 minutes of vigorous activity (e.g., running) weekly, plus muscle-strengthening exercises twice a week.

2

Healthy Diet

Focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Limit sodium to 2,300 mg/day, added sugars, and saturated fats. Consider the Mediterranean or DASH diet.

3

Tobacco Cessation

Quit smoking and avoid secondhand smoke. After one year of quitting, your risk of heart disease drops by 50%. Seek support from helplines or programs.

4

Limit Alcohol Consumption

Drink in moderation: up to one drink per day for women, two for men. Excessive alcohol raises blood pressure and contributes to heart disease.

5

Stress Management

Practice mindfulness, meditation, deep breathing, or yoga. Chronic stress increases cortisol, impacting blood pressure and heart health.

Practical Prevention Tips

Incorporate these simple, actionable tips into your daily routine to protect yourself from NCDs and enhance your quality of life.

Stay Active

Take a 30-minute walk daily, use stairs instead of elevators, or join a local fitness class to boost heart health and energy levels.

Eat Smart

Swap processed snacks for fruits, reduce salt by cooking at home, and aim for 5 servings of vegetables daily to support your body.

Manage Stress

Try 10 minutes of deep breathing, meditate before bed, or practice gratitude to lower stress and protect against hypertension.

Take the Next Step

Prevention starts with awareness and action. Assess your risk, track your progress, and consult a healthcare provider for tailored guidance.
